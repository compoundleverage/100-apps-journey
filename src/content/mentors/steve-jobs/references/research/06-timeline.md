# Agent 6 — Timeline of Steve Jobs

## Summary

Three acts. **Act I (1955–1985):** the autodidact-founder. Adoption, Reed dropout, calligraphy, India, Atari, garage — Apple II (1977), Xerox PARC (1979), Mac (1984), ouster (1985). Taste and reality-distortion fully formed but undisciplined. **Act II (1985–1997):** the wilderness. NeXT teaches him taste without traction is bankruptcy; Pixar teaches him to back genius rather than override it. Pixar IPO (Nov 1995, a week after *Toy Story*) rebuilds confidence. **Act III (1997–2011):** the executive. "1,000 nos" focus, the four-quadrant matrix, iMac → iPod → iTunes Store → iPhone → App Store → iPad. Cancer diagnosis (Oct 2003) injects mortality into every later decision; Stanford 2005 is its clearest articulation. The final 12 months center on succession, Apple Park, iCloud, and the Isaacson biography.

## Intellectual Inflection Points

- **1979 (PARC) — Aesthetic-as-strategy.** Before: hobbyist computer co. After: GUI as moral imperative; Lisa/Mac follow. **Cross-ref:** Agents 2, 5.
- **1985 (Ouster) — Failure as reset.** "The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner" (Stanford 2005). NeXT and Pixar would not exist otherwise. **Cross-ref:** Agents 1, 4.
- **1995 (Pixar IPO) — Curator self-image.** Before: lone auteur. After: backs genius (Lasseter, Catmull); reshapes how he runs Apple post-1997. **Cross-ref:** Agent 3.
- **1997 (Return; product matrix) — Focus as religion.** 350+ SKUs → 4-quadrant matrix. The "1,000 nos" doctrine. **Cross-ref:** Agents 2, 5.
- **2001 (iPod) — Apple as a services company in hardware clothing.** Digital-hub strategy; iTunes is the wedge that makes iPhone thinkable. **Cross-ref:** Agent 2.
- **2003–2004 (Cancer) — Mortality as decision filter.** Stanford 2005: "Death is very likely the single best invention of life." **Cross-ref:** Agents 1, 4.
- **2007 (iPhone) — Cannibalize-yourself doctrine.** Willing to destroy own franchise before competitors do. **Cross-ref:** Agent 2.
- **2010 (iPad + "Thoughts on Flash") — Post-PC manifesto; open-letter as policy lever.** **Cross-ref:** Agent 5.

## Last 12 Months (Oct 2010 – Oct 2011)

The most calibrated window — choices stripped of optionality.

- **Oct–Dec 2010:** Returns from prior leave; oversees Verizon iPhone (announced Jan 11, 2011) and iPad 2. Intensive Isaacson interviews continue.
- **Jan 17, 2011:** Email to staff — medical leave, no return date. Cook handles operations; Jobs retains "major strategic decisions."
- **Mar 2, 2011:** Surprise in-person iPad 2 launch. "I just didn't want to miss today." Frames 2011 as "the year of the iPad 2."
- **Spring–Summer 2011:** Final Isaacson interviews in his Palo Alto living room. Tells Isaacson he wants the book so his kids will know him. Insists he will "get to the next lily pad."
- **Jun 6, 2011:** Final Stevenote, WWDC — iCloud, iOS 5, OS X Lion. Frames iCloud as "demoting the PC and Mac to just be a device."
- **Jun 7, 2011:** Cupertino City Council — pitches Apple Park ("the best office building in the world"). Last public talk.
- **Aug 24, 2011:** Resignation letter. "I hereby resign as CEO of Apple… I would like to serve, if the Board sees fit, as Chairman." Recommends Cook.
- **Sept 2011:** Works from home; advising Cook; per Isaacson, claims to have "finally cracked" the TV.
- **Oct 4, 2011:** iPhone 4S announced by Cook, Forstall, Schiller. Jobs at home, hours from death; Apple had alerted Palo Alto police.
- **Oct 5, 2011:** Dies at home. Mona Simpson reports last words: "Oh wow. Oh wow. Oh wow."
- **Oct 24, 2011:** Isaacson biography published; Jobs had taken no editorial control beyond the cover.

Final-year priorities: (1) succession, (2) Apple Park as institutional legacy, (3) iCloud as post-PC infrastructure, (4) the biography as personal legacy, (5) Apple TV as unfinished bet.

## Notes & Contradictions

- **2003 diagnosis:** sources vary; Isaacson fixes it at Oct 2003 — accepted.
- **9-month delay before surgery:** widely reported; Jobs expressed regret to Isaacson. Medical commentators disagree whether earlier surgery would have changed outcome.
- **NeXT acquisition price:** $400M / $427M / $429M depending on stock + debt; Apple's Dec 20, 1996 announcement said "approximately $400 million."
- **"I finally cracked it" (TV):** Isaacson-only; no contemporaneous corroboration (P=2).
- **First Apple Store:** Tysons Corner & Glendale opened same day (May 19, 2001); some sources credit only one.

## Information Sparsity

- **1976–1980 personal life:** thin first-person record; mostly Wozniak/Markkula.
- **1985–1989 NeXT internals:** few dated Jobs statements; Sculley/Tribble retrospectives dominate.
- **1991–1996 Pixar period:** Jobs publicly quiet; mostly Catmull's *Creativity, Inc.* and Lasseter.
- **2008–2009 health:** Apple disclosures contradicted by later reporting.
- **Jan–Aug 2011:** Isaacson is the dominant primary source — much is single-sourced.
