# Agent 1 — Writings of Steve Jobs

## Summary

Jobs published no books. His "writings" are a thin but unusually load-bearing corpus: a handful of public open letters (Thoughts on Music 2007, Thoughts on Flash 2010), one canonical commencement script (Stanford 2005), brief but consequential press-release statements (Newton EOL 1998, August 2011 resignation letter, the 1985 resignation), keynote scripts he heavily edited but rarely signed, and a large body of internal Apple emails surfaced through litigation (anti-poaching, Epic v. Apple, In re High-Tech Employee). The Steve Jobs Archive's *Make Something Wonderful* (2023) is the most curated published collection of his speeches, drafts, and personal notes, edited by Leslie Berlin and designed by LoveFrom.

The strongest evidence sits in the open letters and Stanford speech (verbatim, dated, on apple.com or stanford.edu, multiply mirrored). The weakest sits in keynote authorship attribution (Jobs edited heavily but had collaborators — direct authorship is inference, not fact) and in second-hand reports of internal memos via Isaacson's biography.

## Self-Coined Phrases / Concepts

- **"Insanely great"** — first public use Jan 24/30, 1984, Apple shareholders' meeting and Boston Computer Society launch of the Macintosh. Recurring through every product era.
- **"Bicycle for the mind"** — first documented use 1980 Apple presentation; canonized in 1981 Scientific American ad and widely thereafter.
- **"Put a dent in the universe"** (variants: "ding," "make a dent") — origin attributed to early Macintosh team motivation talks (1983–84), per Macworld retrospectives and *Make Something Wonderful*. Not from Stanford 2005 (despite frequent misattribution); the speech is sometimes incorrectly cited.
- **"Stay hungry, stay foolish"** — *quoted* by Jobs at Stanford 2005 from the back cover of the final 1974 *Whole Earth Catalog*; he attributes it explicitly. Frequently misread as his own coinage.
- **"Real artists ship"** — attributed to Jobs internally during the 1983 Macintosh push; cited by Andy Hertzfeld at folklore.org [P=2 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=11].
- **"And one more thing..."** — keynote rhetorical device, used from late-1990s NeXT/Apple keynotes onward; not a written-page coinage but a script signature.

## Notes & Contradictions

- **"Open"**: Jobs in *Thoughts on Music* (2007) advocates open, DRM-free, interoperable formats; in *Thoughts on Flash* (2010) he reframes "open" to mean "open standards" (HTML5/H.264) while defending a tightly closed App Store. The word does the rhetorical work each time, but the position shifts.
- **App Store / FCC**: Apple's 2009 FCC letter says Google Voice "has not been approved... and continues to study it" — Google's reply directly contradicted this, saying Phil Schiller verbally rejected it on July 7, 2009. Jobs as CEO owns this contradiction even if he did not draft the letter.
- **The 1985 resignation letter** (to Apple board / Mike Markkula) and the **August 24, 2011 resignation letter** are tonally inverse: 1985 is wounded and combative; 2011 is gracious and brief ("I have always said if there ever came a day when I could no longer meet my duties..."). Both are verifiably his.
- **"Stay hungry, stay foolish"** is widely attributed to Jobs but he sourced it explicitly to the Whole Earth Catalog. Treating it as Jobs-coined misreads the speech.
