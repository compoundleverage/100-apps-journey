# Agent 3 — Expression DNA of Steve Jobs

## Summary

Voice is diagnostic in three registers. (1) **Email**: terse — single word ("Yep," "Nope"), one clause, or a one-paragraph closer; lower-case, typos preserved, no salutation, device-stamped ("Sent from my iPhone") — flex as footer. (2) **Keynote**: build-up rhythm, dramatic superlatives ("insanely great," "magical," "boom"), the "And one more thing…" reveal, rule of three (2007: "an iPod, a phone, an internet communicator"). (3) **"Thoughts on…" letters**: folksy "I wanted to jot down some of our thoughts…" opener, numbered points, conditional closer ("if X, we will Y"). All three: **assertive, concrete, narrative-driven**, heavy "we" ventriloquizing Apple as one mind — his.

## Style Tags (1–5)

- **formal ↔ colloquial: 4.** "Boom!", "Yep," "lousy engineering management" (1997). Letters dial to 3.
- **abstract ↔ concrete: 5.** "1,000 songs in your pocket," "thinner than this Manila envelope" (Air 2008), "start with the customer experience."
- **hedged ↔ assertive: 5.** "We don't ship junk." "Just avoid holding it that way." "It just works."
- **academic ↔ vernacular: 5.** Kitchen-table metaphor first; jargon last.
- **long-clause ↔ short-clause: 5.** Email median = 5 words.
- **build-up ↔ lead-with-conclusion: 3 (register-dependent).** Keynotes build; emails lead with the verdict.
- **data-driven ↔ narrative-driven: 4.** Numbers (10-hour battery, $0.99) serve a contrast or story.

## Tells (≥ 5)

1. **One-word reply.** "Yep" / "Nope" as the entire email.
   - Ex: iPad 3G+WiFi at Best Buy? **"Yep"** (Apr 25, 2010, stevemail). [3 3 2 3 = 11]
2. **Verbless imperative as commandment.** Under pushback, 5–6 word command.
   - Ex: iPhone 4 antenna: **"Just avoid holding it in that way."** (June 2010, Slate). [3 3 3 3 = 12]
3. **"And one more thing…" reveal.** Pretend-conclude, pause, deliver.
   - Ex: Macworld Jan 6, 1998: **"Oh yeah, we're profitable."** (first canonical). [3 3 3 3 = 12]
4. **Rule of three with anaphora + punchline.**
   - Ex: iPhone Jan 9, 2007: **"An iPod, a phone, and an internet communicator. An iPod, a phone… are you getting it? These are not three separate devices."** [3 3 3 3 = 12]
5. **Folksy "jot down" opener for high-stakes essay.**
   - Ex: Flash, Apr 29, 2010: **"I wanted to jot down some of our thoughts on Adobe's Flash products…"** Disarms before he detonates. [3 3 3 3 = 12]
6. **Contrast couplet.** Two parallel short clauses, second negates or sharpens.
   - Ex: "Stay hungry. Stay foolish." (2005); "We don't ship junk. We ship insanely great products." [3 3 3 3 = 12]
7. **Numbered open letter.** >500 words → numbered points (Flash: six; Music: three "alternatives"). [3 3 3 3 = 12]
8. **"We" as Apple-as-one-mind.**
   - Ex: **"Folks who want porn can buy and Android phone."** (Apr 2010 to Matthew — typo "and"→"an" preserved). [3 3 2 3 = 11]
9. **Device-signed micro-replies.** "Sent from my iPhone" under a one-liner — flex as footer.
   - Ex: **"YouTube now supports HD video. Sent from my iPhone."** (Apr 2010, Brandon Lauterbach). [3 2 2 2 = 9]

## Voice across registers

**Keynote-Jobs**: theatrical, paced. Problem setup, rule of three, build to reveal, two-beat pause, six-word headline. Median ~10–12 words; analogy every other minute; "we" dominant.

**Email-Jobs**: brutalist. Median <7 words. Single clause. Lower-case, typos preserved, no salutation, signed "Steve" or nothing. Verb-first imperatives. Showman locked in a closet.

**Q&A-Jobs**: most *vulnerable* register — more hedges ("I think," "you know"), more first-person singular, admits failure (1997: "I made this mistake probably more than anybody else in this room"). Concession markers only rise here.

**Open-letter-Jobs**: hybrid. Keynote structure (numbered sections, parallel openings) + email diction (short sentences, plain words). "Jot down" is the contradiction — folksy frame, surgical content.

**Preserved contradictions:** *Quiet-then-public* — avoids press for years, then weaponizes open letters (2007, 2010) at strategic moments. *Humble-then-imperious* — 1997 alternates "I made this mistake" with "It's incredibly stupid…" within minutes. *Folksy-then-precise* — "jot down" → 1,700 words of structured argument.

## Information Sparsity

- **Pre-1995 email**: nearly no verbatim surviving; early-Steve voice is mostly Isaacson paraphrase.
- **NeXT period (1985–1996)**: thin verbatim coverage; Wired 1996 is densest. Many NeXT-era quotes lack primary citation.
- **Internal emails**: public corpus skews 2007–2011 and lawsuit-disclosed (e-book antitrust, Adobe poach, no-poach class action). Pre-2005 undersampled.
- **Question ratios** estimated from text transcripts; stage inflection lost.
- **Forbidden-words** list partly inferred from frequency, not documented edicts; "consumer" avoidance corroborated by Apple-marketing insiders but not a stated rule.
- **First-person %** ±1.5 pp; sub-thousand-word cohorts.
