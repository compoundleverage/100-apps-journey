## Decision 3 — Co-founding Initialized Capital (2012-2013); building it through Coinbase, Instacart, Flexport, Opendoor, then leaving in 2022

**Divergence vs. principle:** Tan champions "default alive" but Initialized expanded headcount during the ZIRP era; in October 2024, Brett Gibson laid off Jennifer Wolf, Jenny Fleiss, and others — explicitly framed as "too many layers" that needed shedding. ## Pattern across decisions (load-bearing)

1. **Each major exit/pivot was triggered by his own diagnosis of organizational drift, not external pressure.** Posterous → he should have been CEO. YC departure → wanted dedicated fund control. Initialized step-back → ownership/scale dilution. YC return → institutional renewal. Civic activism → SF needed reform.

2. **He learns from explicit reversal moments and weaponizes the lesson into content.** "I Said the Wrong Thing" essay turns a failure into a teaching artifact. Posterous post-mortem becomes a reusable parable.

3. **He moves from inside operator to public-figure / institution-shaper as his career compounds.** Each role becomes more public; less hands-on coding/building over time.

4. **He acknowledges contradictions selectively.** "I am addicted to yes-and!" tweet (re: focus is saying no) — self-deprecating about the gap. But on the cruelty / die-slow tweet, the apology was about the *form* (rap reference), not the underlying combat.

5. **He does not publicly admit reversal as reversal.** Following the Steve Jobs pattern: when YC's strategy or his own thesis evolves, the new position is reframed as having been the plan.
