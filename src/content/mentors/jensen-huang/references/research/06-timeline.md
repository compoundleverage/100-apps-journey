## Career timeline

### 1963: Birth in Tainan, Taiwan
Born February 17, 1963 in Tainan to Loretta and Bill Huang. Father was a chemical engineer.

### ~1972-1973: Immigration to United States
At about age 9-10, Jensen was sent with his older brother Jeff to the United States, ahead of the rest of the family. They lived briefly with an uncle in Tacoma, WA. Then sent to Oneida Baptist Institute, a boarding school in rural Kentucky — an unusual school originally designed for "troubled" rural kids; Jensen has described scrubbing toilets there. He has said this period of suffering shaped him profoundly.

### Mid-1970s: Family reunited in Oregon
Family eventually moved to Oregon. Jensen attended Aloha High School. Played table tennis at high level (was reportedly nationally-ranked in his age group at one point).

### 1984: BS Electrical Engineering, Oregon State University
Graduated. Met Lori Mills (his future wife) in his freshman engineering class.

### Mid-1980s: AMD, then LSI Logic
Worked as a chip designer at AMD, then microprocessor designer at LSI Logic. Married Lori. Two children: Spencer and Madison.

### 1992: MS Electrical Engineering, Stanford
Earned masters from Stanford while working full-time.

### 1993: Co-founded NVIDIA at the Denny's in San Jose
Founded with Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem. Famous origin: a Denny's booth. Nvidia incorporated April 5, 1993.

### 1995: NV1 launch (failure)
First chip launched. Used quadratic-texture-mapping; market chose triangle rasterization (Microsoft DirectX). Commercial disaster.

### 1996-1997: Near-death, then RIVA pivot
~30% layoffs. Pivot to triangle rasterization. RIVA 128 (1997) saved the company. Sega contract failed. Jensen's "30 days from going out of business" mantra dates from this period.

### 1999: GeForce 256 launch — "the world's first GPU"
Coined the term "GPU." IPO on January 22, 1999.

### 2000-2002: Dot-com survival
Nvidia survived. Stock cratered but the company did not.

### 2006: CUDA launches
General-purpose computing on GPUs. The bet that defines the next 20 years. Wall Street did not understand it.

### 2007: Tesla architecture (first explicitly compute-focused GPU)

### 2008: Financial crisis + chip recall
Lost ~$200M on a chip-packaging defect. Survived another near-death.

### 2012: AlexNet uses GPUs
Hinton, Krizhevsky, Sutskever's AlexNet runs on Nvidia GPUs. Deep learning era begins. Jensen subsequently positions Nvidia as the deep-learning infrastructure provider.

### 2016: Pascal architecture; first DGX-1 hand-delivered to OpenAI
Jensen personally hand-delivers the first DGX-1 to OpenAI (with Elon Musk and Sam Altman). Begins ritual of personal supercomputer delivery to era-defining customers.

### 2017: Volta + Tensor Cores
First GPU with dedicated AI matrix units. GTC 2017 explicitly names "the era of AI."

### 2019: Mellanox acquisition ($6.9B)
Networking integrated into the stack. One of the best M&A decisions of the 2010s.

### 2020: Ampere architecture
COVID-era kitchen-keynote. "Ampere is in the oven."

### 2020-2022: ARM acquisition attempt and graceful failure
$40B deal collapsed February 2022 under regulatory pressure.

### 2022: Hopper architecture (H100)
GTC 2022 keynote — "Moore's Law has ended" manifesto. 4-hour keynote.

### November 2022: ChatGPT launches
World shifts. Nvidia is the picks-and-shovels supplier.

### March 2023: GTC keynote — "AI's iPhone moment"
Jensen coins the phrase. Stock 3x's over the next 12 months.

### September 2023: Acquired podcast (3.5 hours)
The single densest interview ever recorded with Jensen.

### November 2023: 60 Minutes profile
Mainstream U.S. recognition arrives.

### March 2024: Stanford GSB View From The Top — "I wish suffering on you"
Famous speech. Suffering-as-character-formation enters the lexicon.

### March 2024: GTC 2024 keynote — Blackwell architecture announced
Held the Blackwell board aloft. Sovereign AI as commercial frame.

### June 2024: Computex Taipei keynote
Returns to Taiwan as the most-watched figure at the conference. Introduces the "blueprint" for AI factories.

### Late 2024: Nvidia briefly the world's most valuable company
Crosses Apple in market cap repeatedly through 2024.

### November 2024: Tae Kim, *The Nvidia Way*, published
First major biography during Jensen's lifetime as a dominant figure.

### January 2025: CES keynote (Las Vegas)
Cosmos world model unveiled. Project DIGITS personal AI computer. Robotics emphasis.

### March 2025: GTC 2025 — Blackwell Ultra + Rubin roadmap
Future generations of architecture announced. Robotics platform GR00T expanded. Sovereign AI deals formalized with multiple governments (UAE, Saudi Arabia, France, India, Japan, Korea, Taiwan).

## LAST 12 MONTHS (≈ May 2025 — May 2026)

This is the section the persona-forge skill mandates be specifically covered. As of research date 2026-05-09:

### Mid-2025: Sovereign AI deals expand
Nvidia announced or formalized infrastructure partnerships with multiple sovereign-AI initiatives — including Saudi Arabia (G42-style infrastructure), UAE, France, Japan, India, South Korea, and a number of European countries. Jensen frames this as "every country needs to own its own intelligence infrastructure."

### China and export controls (2025)
Nvidia continues to navigate U.S. export controls. Modified chips (H20-successor, B20-class) designed specifically for China market. Jensen has been publicly more vocal about the costs of decoupling. Trade-related earnings impact has been quantified in 10-Q filings.

### Robotics keynotes
Jensen has positioned 2025-2026 as "the robotics decade" — analogous to how 2017-2022 was the AI training decade and 2022-2025 was the inference deployment decade. Cosmos, GR00T, and Project DIGITS form the robotics platform stack.

### Stargate involvement
Nvidia is reportedly the silicon supplier for the OpenAI/Oracle/SoftBank "Stargate" data-center program announced January 2025. Jensen has publicly endorsed the framing of "AI as national infrastructure."

### Succession commentary
Jensen has been asked repeatedly about retirement and succession. His standing answer: "I have no plans to retire. I love what I do." He is approximately 62-63 years old and reports working harder than ever. Internal succession planning at Nvidia is opaque; presumed candidates include Colette Kress (CFO), Manuvir Das, Bryan Catanzaro, and Bill Dally.

### Personal richest-individual moments
Jensen has periodically been listed among the world's wealthiest individuals as Nvidia stock has appreciated. He has not changed his public posture about wealth — continues to wear the leather jacket, not pose, talks about Nvidia as if it could die at any moment.

### Public talks
- CES 2025 (January)
- GTC 2025 (March)
- GTC Taipei 2025 (June)
- Computex 2025 (June)
- Multiple government-summit appearances

### Books and external coverage
Tae Kim's *The Nvidia Way* (November 2024) remains the dominant external account. No major new biography has appeared since.

### Health and personal
Jensen has acknowledged on multiple occasions his "terrible memory" — a self-deprecating tic that has stayed consistent. No public health concerns. Continues to work in the Santa Clara HQ daily.

### Cultural status
By 2026, Jensen is unambiguously the dominant figure of the AI accelerator era. Comparison to Steve Jobs is now standard journalistic shorthand. Comparison to Andy Grove is the more precise analytical comparison.
