# Agent 1 — Writings

Focus: Jensen Huang's own written corpus. Unlike most CEOs, Jensen's "writing" is heavily oral — his most-quoted lines come from keynotes (which Nvidia transcribes), all-hands meetings, and shareholder letters. He is not a Twitter intellectual, has no book, has no Substack. His written canon is shareholder letters + the GTC keynote scripts (which Nvidia publishes verbatim) + the Stanford 2024 commencement speech + the 30 Years of NVIDIA anniversary speech (2023).

## Core corpus (scored)

### Annual letters to shareholders (Nvidia 10-K filings, FY2014–FY2024)
URL: https://investor.nvidia.com/financial-info/financial-reports/
[score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

The "Letter from the CEO" appears at the front of every Nvidia annual report. Length varies, ~500-2000 words. Single most-underread Jensen primary source. Recurring frame: Nvidia is not a chip company; it is "the engine of accelerated computing." The phrase "accelerated computing" appears in essentially every letter from FY2017 onward.

Recurring themes (≥3 letters):
- "We are 30 days from going out of business" (paraphrased) — Jensen's standing internal mantra; reflected in shareholder letters as urgency framing even at peak success.
- "End of Moore's Law" / "Dennard scaling broken" — appears in FY2017, FY2019, FY2021, FY2023 letters as the structural reason Nvidia exists.
- "Full-stack" — CPU + GPU + DPU + networking + software + services + AI factories. Each layer treated as part of one architecture.
- "AI factories" (FY2023, FY2024 letters): the new framing for data centers as production facilities producing tokens.
- "Sovereign AI" (FY2024): countries must own their own intelligence infrastructure.

### "30 Years of NVIDIA" speech (2023, internal all-hands released publicly)
[score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

Anniversary address. Recurrent themes: Nvidia survived three near-death experiences (NV1 1995-1996, dot-com 2000-2002, 2008 financial crisis). Each time, "we were 30 days from going out of business." The cultural fuel of perpetual urgency is explicitly named here — "the company that operates as if it might die tomorrow is the one that lives forever."

Key claim: "We invented accelerated computing." Not "we invented the GPU" — the framing is paradigm-not-product. CUDA is treated as the inflection point, not the GeForce 256.

### Stanford GSB Commencement / View From The Top (2024)
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qXDdToZHzE-style talks (Stanford GSB View From The Top series, March 2024)
[score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

The "I wish suffering on you" speech. Famous line: "To this day I use the phrase 'pain and suffering' inside our company with great glee. I mean that with great glee. Boy, this is going to cause a lot of pain and suffering. And I mean that in a happy way, because you want to train, you want to refine the character of your company. You want greatness out of them, and greatness is not intelligence, as you know. Greatness comes from character. And character isn't formed out of smart people. It's formed out of people who suffered."

Sub-themes:
- "I hope upon you ample doses of pain and suffering."
- "Lower expectations for greatness." (Counter-intuitive: students with high expectations are fragile.)
- "I have low expectations for myself, as a result every day is a gift."
- Suffering as character formation, not deprivation.

### GTC Keynote scripts (Nvidia publishes annually)
URL: https://www.nvidia.com/gtc/keynote/
[score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

The keynote is Jensen's structured annual policy document. Pattern across 2017-2025:
- 2017 GTC ("Tensor Cores, Volta") — first explicit naming of "the era of AI"
- 2018 GTC — Turing, RTX, the "ray tracing is here" demo
- 2020 GTC keynote (filmed in his kitchen due to COVID) — "Ampere is here, and it's in the oven."
- 2022 GTC ("Hopper") — the accelerated-computing manifesto. Explicit framing: "Moore's Law has ended. Accelerated computing is the path forward." 4-hour keynote.
- 2023 GTC ("AI's iPhone moment") — coined the phrase that became universal; positioned ChatGPT as analogous to 2007 iPhone moment.
- 2024 GTC ("Blackwell") — AI factories, sovereign AI, robotics with GR00T.
- 2025 GTC — Blackwell Ultra, Rubin roadmap, Cosmos world model.

Recurring rhetorical moves (≥4 keynotes):
- "Buy more, save more" — the gag where bigger purchases yield more savings (often delivered laughing at himself for the reductive logic).
- "The more you buy, the more you save."
- The "demo as incantation" pattern: walks audience through a live demo, explaining what's happening in real time, often unscripted asides.
- Rule of three product unveilings: "It does X. It does Y. It does Z."
- Hardware-as-jewelry physical reveals (Hopper held aloft, Blackwell board lifted from podium).
- The "$X away from going out of business" as recurring joke and serious frame.

## Self-coined terms / repurposed concepts

- **"Accelerated computing"** — the central frame. Not Jensen's invention (the term predates him in scientific computing) but he repurposed it as the unifying corporate paradigm, against "general-purpose computing" (CPUs).
- **"AI factory"** — production facility producing tokens. Coined ~2022-2023. Replaces "data center" in Nvidia's lexicon.
- **"Sovereign AI"** — countries needing their own intelligence infrastructure. Coined late 2023; politically loaded and strategically commercial.
- **"AI's iPhone moment"** — coined by Jensen at GTC March 2023, immediately became universal in tech press.
- **"The Earth's most important computer"** — describing the AI supercomputer, GTC 2024.
- **"GeForce was the engine that brought us into AI"** — recurring origin myth framing.

## Books or thinkers Jensen cites approvingly

Jensen rarely cites books. When he does:
- **Andy Grove, *Only the Paranoid Survive*** — cited multiple times; the 30-days-from-going-out-of-business framing has direct lineage.
- **Clayton Christensen** (*Innovator's Dilemma*) — cited regarding self-disruption and the 1996 RIVA pivot away from NV1.
- **Geoffrey Moore** (*Crossing the Chasm*) — cited in early-Nvidia context.
- **Pixar / Catmull's *Creativity Inc.*** — cited approvingly re: brain trust and feedback culture.

He does not cite philosophers, economists, or business school cases. His intellectual lineage is operating-CEOs-who-survived (Grove, especially) and engineering managers.
