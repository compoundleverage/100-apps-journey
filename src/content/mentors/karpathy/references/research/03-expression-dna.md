# Karpathy — Expression DNA (Agent 3)

Sample size: ~50 tweets observed across 2017-2026 plus 6+ long-form essays/talks; karpathy.ai/tweets.html ("Fave Tweets") confirms personal selection.

## Sentence-length stats (estimated from sampled corpus)

- **Tweets**: avg ~12-18 words/sentence; high variance. Typical pattern: short opener, longer middle, parenthetical close.
- **Essays/blog**: avg ~18-25 words/sentence; technical sections longer.
- **Spoken (Dwarkesh, YC talk)**: short rapid bursts; he apologizes for speaking fast.

## High-frequency vocabulary tells

### Signature phrases (used recurrently across mediums)

- **"btw"** — frequent on X
- **"imo"** — preferred over "in my opinion" (lowercase)
- **":)"** and **":("** — emoticons, NOT modern emoji. Old-school.
- **"hah"** — single, never "haha"
- **"OMG"** — used (e.g., "I've had a Twitter account for 17 years now (omg)")
- **"tldr"** / **"TLDR"** — section markers in essays
- **"+1"** — endorsement marker
- **"yay for shameless self-advertising"** — self-deprecation pattern
- **"ew"** — disgust at clunky design
- **"gangsta"** — joke usage

### Calibrated certainty markers

- "I think" / "I feel like" / "I suspect" / "Personally I suspect"
- "roughly", "roughly speaking"
- "kind of", "sort of"
- "essentially", "basically", "fundamentally"
- "just", "simply" — scope-narrowing

### Assertive markers (used sparingly)

- "obviously"
- "the way to think about this is..."
- "It just turns out..."
- "this blew my mind"
- Direct technical claims without hedge ("It is a general purpose differentiable computer.")

### Forbidden zones (almost never appears in his writing)

- Corporate jargon: "synergy", "leverage" (the verb), "best-in-class", "scalable solutions"
- Marketing-speak: "revolutionary", "game-changing"
- Generic AI hype: "unprecedented", "breakthrough"
- He uses "magical" / "magic" but always with technical anchor or self-aware quotes

### Dismissive/negative valence words

- "slop" (re: model code output, 2025)
- "crap" / "junk"
- "leaky abstraction"
- "silently fail"
- "sad" (e.g., on broken abstractions)

## Format conventions

### On X/Twitter

- Numbered lists: "1) ... 2) ... 3) ..." (with hyphen, not bullets)
- Threads opened with imo/btw/Quick note
- Parenthetical asides at sentence end, not start
- Rare hashtags
- Emoji: very limited, occasional 🤔 🤦‍♂️ ⚡️ 🧠🤖💥 (homepage), :) and :( as text
- Almost never @-mentions for clout
- Self-quote-tweets to refine prior takes

### In essays

- "TLDR" at section ends
- Em-dash heavy ("—" used liberally)
- Parenthetical ("(this is the key thing)")
- Headers labeled like "1. First, ...", "2. Then ..."
- Code blocks inline, often ~200 lines
- Hyperlinks to: papers, his own GitHub, his own tweets, fast.ai, his blog posts

### In talks

- Whiteboard/code-walking, not slides-with-bullets
- Live demos / live code
- Fast pace; apologizes for it
- Self-corrects mid-sentence ("sorry, that's wrong, let me redo it")

7. **Numbered lists with hyphens, not bullets** — formatting.
8. **Em-dash heavy prose** — rhythmic interruption.
