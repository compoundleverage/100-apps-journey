## Canonical primary writings

### "How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" — Twitter thread, May 31, 2018
- URL: https://x.com/naval/status/1002103360646823936 ; https://nav.al/rich
- 40-tweet structured framework. Naval's most-cited corpus.
- [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **Recurring claims** (each appearing ≥3 times across his corpus):
  - "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep."
  - "You're not going to get rich renting out your time."
  - "You will get rich by giving society what it wants but does not yet know how to get. At scale."
  - "Pick an industry where you can play long-term games with long-term people."
  - "Apply specific knowledge, with leverage, and eventually you will get what you deserve."
  - Four kinds of luck: blind / hustle / preparation / character.
  - "Fortunes require leverage." (Code, media, capital, labor.)
  - "Code and media are permissionless leverage. They're the leverage behind the newly rich."
  - "Read what you love until you love to read." (Curiosity over curriculum.)
  - "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."

### "Arm Yourself With Specific Knowledge" — nav.al essay
- URL: https://nav.al/specific-knowledge
- [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=2 total=11]
- **Definition (verbatim register):** "Specific knowledge is knowledge that you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else and replace you."
- **Mechanism:** "Specific knowledge is found by pursuing your genuine curiosity and passion rather than whatever is hot right now."
- **Diagnostic:** "Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like work to others."
- **Discovery rule:** Look at what you already do that "feels like play to you and looks like work to others" — that is your specific knowledge.

### "Play Long-Term Games With Long-Term People" — nav.al
- URL: https://nav.al/long-term
- [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **Core line (recurring at least 5 times in corpus):** "Play long-term games with long-term people. All returns in life, whether in wealth, relationships, or knowledge, come from compound interest."
- **Mechanism argument:** Iterated games solve prisoner's dilemma; trust accrues only when participants expect future dealings.
- **Examples used:** Warren Buffett (stable platform), Senate vs House (longer terms enable cooperation), Elad Gil ("when the deal is being done, he will bend over backward to give me extra").
- **Economic frame:** Long-term games are positive-sum. "We're all baking the pie together." Short-term is "cutting up the pie."

### "Happiness Is Learned" / "Happiness" — nav.al
- URL: https://nav.al/happiness
- [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **Core thesis:** "Happiness is the state when nothing is missing." It is a default state revealed by removing desire, not an achievement.
- **Verbatim:** "Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want."
- **Verbatim:** "Peace is happiness at rest. Happiness is peace in motion."
- **Buddhist register:** "The peace that we seek is not peace of mind, it's peace from mind." (Used on JRE #1309 verbatim; appears in essays.)
- **Stoic register:** "Reality is neutral. How you interpret it is up to you."
- **Anti-self-help move:** "You cannot work toward peace, only understanding."
- **Hedonic adaptation claim:** "Hedonic adaptation is more powerful for man-made things than for natural things."

### "Escape Competition Through Authenticity" — nav.al
- URL: https://nav.al/competition-authenticity
- [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=2 total=11]
- **Core line:** "No one can compete with you on being you."
- **Mechanism:** Pre-internet, the advice would have been useless; post-internet, you can build a permission-less audience around what only you can do.
- **Diagnostic:** "When you see a lot of competition, sometimes that indicates the masses have already arrived."

## Self-coined / repurposed terms

- **"Specific knowledge"** — Naval's coinage. Pre-Naval the phrase had a different (Hayekian) usage. In his usage: knowledge that cannot be mass-trained.
- **"Permissionless leverage"** — repurposed from blockchain vocabulary; applied to media and code.
- **"Rational Buddhism"** — his self-label for his blend of evolutionary psychology + Buddhist practice + Stoic ethics.
- **"Status games are zero-sum, wealth games are positive-sum"** — distinct framing he repeats verbatim.

## Intellectual lineage cited approvingly

- **Stoics** (Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius — repeatedly cited on equanimity, control)
- **Buddhists** (Buddha, Vipassana tradition — cited on desire and default state)
- **Lao Tzu / Tao Te Ching** (cited via aphorism style)
- **Charlie Munger** (mental models, inversion)

Notably absent: contemporary tech founders, business gurus, or public intellectuals other than Taleb. He cites *out* of his own field deliberately.
