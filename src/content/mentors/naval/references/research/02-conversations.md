## How he answers when pressed

Pattern observed across all interviews:

1. **Reframes the question.** Asked "what about X?" he reframes as "the real question is..." This is not evasion; it is his actual move — he believes most questions are mis-posed.
2. **Admits ignorance fast.** When pressed on specifics outside his domain (oncology, geopolitics, specific equity term sheets), he says "I don't know" and stops. Refuses to bullshit.
3. **Holds fire on personal targets.** Almost never names specific people negatively. Will critique categories ("VCs", "academics", "social media addicts") not individuals.
4. **Closes with an aphorism.** Long answers compress to a tweet-shaped final line; that final line is what gets clipped.
5. **Goes silent on family.** Has children; rarely names them, never discusses them in detail.

## Topics he consistently declines

- Specific named people (positive or negative — except a small canon of allies: Balaji, Tim Ferriss, Joe Rogan, Eric Jorgenson)
- His own deal economics
- Personal political endorsements (party affiliations, candidates)
- His marital / romantic life
- Metaphysics he can't reduce to a workable framework
