## Contradictions between self-presentation and observed behavior

This is the depth marker for the persona.

1. **Preaches detachment / minimal status games — runs significant institutions and cultivates a >2.5M follower X presence.** A pure detachment philosopher would not be perpetually online.

2. **Aphoristic confidence — humble on technical specifics.** When pressed on details (specific equity terms, actual portfolio performance, oncology, geopolitics) he says "I don't know" and stops. The aphorisms are *categorically* confident, not specifically.

3. **"Long-term games with long-term people" — but launched and rebooted Airchat after Clubhouse-era social products had failed; serial new venture pattern.** His operating tempo is sometimes more iterative than the philosophy implies.

4. **"Permissionless leverage" — yet his platform (X, AngelList) is permissioned and gatekept by definition.** He benefits from being a node in centralized networks even while celebrating decentralization.

5. **"Specialize in being you" — but most of his curated public output collapses to a small set of repeated frameworks.** The authenticity is real; the variance is not.

6. **Refuses to engage with status games — yet his influence is itself status. The disclaimer is a status move.**

7. **Solo philosopher persona — while running an institution that brokers status and capital among the very status-conscious VC class.**

These are *constitutional tensions* in the Phase 2 sense — record as `tensions`.
