## Decision 1 — Founding Epinions (1999) and the litigation aftermath (2003-2005)

- **Action:** Co-founded Epinions, an early consumer-review site. Raised $45M VC. Merged with Dealtime (2003) to form Shopping.com. Naval and co-founder Nirav Tolia later sued Benchmark Capital, August Capital, and the co-founder over the merger terms in 2005, alleging fraud — settled December 2005.
- **Source:** Wikipedia + tech press coverage [score: P=1 V=3 A=2 C=2 total=8]
- **Stated reasoning:** Naval has discussed this on multiple podcasts (Ferriss #97 most explicitly): the experience shaped his later founder-side bias and the AngelList founding thesis.
- **Reflection later:** "The reason I started AngelList was because I'd been on the founder side of these deals and I'd seen how the information asymmetry worked." (Paraphrase from Ferriss #97; cross-referenced in Almanack.)
- **Pattern revealed:** Naval is willing to litigate when stakes warrant; he is not the pure detached philosopher his later persona suggests.

## Decision 2 — Founding AngelList (2010)

- **Action:** Co-founded AngelList with Babak Nivi as a free platform connecting startups with angels. Started as a blog ("Venture Hacks", 2007).
- **Stated reasoning:** Reduce the gatekeeping power of traditional VCs; democratize access to early-stage capital. Information asymmetry was Naval's named target.
- **Outcome:** AngelList scaled into the dominant US angel platform; spawned Republic, AngelList Talent (Wellfound), and the syndicate model. Became a $4B+ valued company.
- **Source:** AngelList blog + multiple long-form interviews [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **Pattern revealed:** Naval as institution-builder. The "permissionless" rhetoric started here — but the platform itself became permissioned in practice (deal access, accreditation rules).

## Decision 3 — Spearhead launch (2017) and the operator-angel thesis

- **Action:** Launched Spearhead — gave operating founders $1M each to deploy as angel investors.
- **Stated reasoning:** Operators have specific knowledge VCs don't; equipping them with capital creates a new class of high-quality early backers.
- **Outcome:** Spearhead-backed companies include Neuralink, Opendoor, Rippling. Cohort 3 raised $100M.
- **Source:** AngelList + tech press [score: P=2 V=3 A=2 C=2 total=9]
- **Pattern revealed:** Specific knowledge × leverage applied institutionally. Same framework deployed at platform level.

## Decision 4 — The "How to Get Rich" tweet thread (May 31, 2018)

- **Action:** Posted 40-tweet thread that became viral and re-defined his public persona.
- **Stated reasoning (per Network State Conference 2024):** Wanted to compress his startup advice into a permanent compounding asset. The thread itself is permission-less media leverage — the practice exemplifies the theory.
- **Outcome:** 21,000+ retweets initially; turned into a book, an audio essay, countless derivative content; established him as a public philosopher.
- **Source:** Twitter + Network State 2024 [score: P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **Pattern revealed:** Naval *practices what he preaches* on permissionless leverage — the thread is the proof.

## Decision 5 — Public crypto/Bitcoin advocacy (~2013 onward) and the calmer 2024-2025 evolution

- **Action:** Public Bitcoin / Ethereum bull from earliest credible VC stage. Founded MetaStable Capital crypto hedge fund in 2014. Tweeted heavily about crypto through cycles.
- **Stated reasoning:** Crypto = permissionless capital + permissionless code = pure leverage. Sound-money, anti-fiat thesis.
- **Evolution:** Through the 2022-2024 cycle, Naval's public crypto-bullishness *cooled* into more philosophical pragmatism. He no longer evangelizes specific coins; speaks more about the structural thesis (decentralization, sound money) than tactical advice.
- **Source:** Twitter timeline + Tim Ferriss #473 (2020 crypto strategy episode) [score: P=3 V=3 A=2 C=2 total=10]
- **Pattern revealed:** Naval revises *tone* without retracting *thesis*. He changes his mind in degree, not kind. Critics call this hedging; defenders call it calibrated update.

## Decision 6 — Airchat launch (2023) and relaunch (April 2024)

- **Action:** With Brian Norgard (ex-Tinder), built Airchat — voice-first asynchronous social app. Launched 2023; relaunched April 2024 to thousands of downloads, briefly top-25 in US App Store.
- **Stated reasoning:** Voice is more intimate than text; threaded async voice is a new category between text and live audio.
- **Outcome:** Initial buzz; long-term traction unclear by 2025-2026. By the most recent public commentary, Airchat had not become a dominant platform — but Naval has not declared it a failure.
- **Source:** TechCrunch April 2024, podcastnotes [score: P=2 V=3 A=2 C=2 total=9]
- **Pattern revealed:** Naval *builds* despite the philosophical posture. He has not retired into pure philosophy at age 50+. The Airchat launch contradicts the strict "step out of the game" reading of his philosophy.

## Decision 7 — Public political stance shifts (selective)

- **Action:** Naval tweets *selectively* but pointedly on political/cultural topics. Publicly:
  - Pro high-skill legal immigration (consistent across decade)
  - Anti-regulatory-capture (especially in AI, crypto)
  - Generally libertarian-coded but refuses party affiliation
  - Recent (2025): Pro-tariff *if* designed for network-effect industries (a notable thesis update)
  - Critical of "wokeness" as a status game (mid-decade tweets)
- **Pattern revealed:** Politically selective, not party-loyal. Will surprise both wings.
- **Source:** Twitter timeline + All-In E215 [score: P=3 V=3 A=2 C=2 total=10]

## Decision 8 — The deliberate aphoristic register choice

- **Action:** Naval *chose* aphoristic short-form as his public register, despite being capable of long-form essays. This is a stylistic decision with strategic consequences.
- **Stated reasoning (Knowledge Project + Almanack):** Tweets compound; tweets are permission-less media leverage; tweets force compression which forces clarity.
- **Pattern revealed:** Naval treats his own corpus as a leverage portfolio. The aphorisms are infrastructure.

## Cases where stated principles and actions diverged

1. **"Step out of the game entirely"** vs. running AngelList, Spearhead, MetaStable, Airchat. The detachment is rhetorical; the operating cadence is high.

2. **"Networking is mostly waste"** vs. cultivated relationships with Tim Ferriss, Joe Rogan, Balaji, Eric Jorgenson, the All-In hosts — each of which became his amplification channel. Networks worked very well for Naval; he has not amended the claim.

3. **"Specialize in being you"** vs. clear evolution from: dot-com founder → litigant → venture-hacks blogger → AngelList CEO → philosopher → AI/crypto commentator → builder again (Airchat). The "self" he has specialized in has reinvented multiple times.

4. **"Long-term games with long-term people"** vs. the 2005 litigation against his Epinions co-founder and investors. The principle came later; the breach came first. He has not relitigated this on podcasts but it is part of the record.

5. **"Most education is socialization"** vs. having sent his children to private schools and reading constantly himself. The escape-hatch advice is for the principled minority; the actual operating mode is mainstream.

6. **"Anti-status"** vs. the very cultivated maintenance of his public image. His refusal to engage in status-games is itself a status-game.

These divergences are the *constitutional tensions* (Phase 2.4) — they should be preserved as `tensions`, not airbrushed.
