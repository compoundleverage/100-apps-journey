# Agent 6 — Paul Graham: Timeline

## Birth → today

- **1964** — Born November 13, in Weymouth, England. Family moves to Pittsburgh, PA when PG is young.
- **1968–1972** — Childhood in Pittsburgh. Father a physicist; mother a homemaker.
- **1986** — BA, Cornell University (philosophy).
- **1988** — MS, Harvard University (computer science).
- **1990** — PhD, Harvard University (computer science). Thesis on programming language design (specifically: applicative programming with Lisp/Scheme).
- **Early 1990s** — Studies painting at Rhode Island School of Design (RISD) and L'Accademia di Belle Arti di Firenze, Florence. The painting period is heavily reflected in *Hackers & Painters* (the title essay frames programming as art-adjacent).
- **1993** — Publishes *On Lisp* (Prentice Hall). Considered a definitive Lisp text on macros.
- **1995** — Publishes *ANSI Common Lisp* (Prentice Hall, with technical-reference appendix).
- **1995** — Co-founds **Viaweb** with Robert Morris and Trevor Blackwell. First commercial web app for online stores. Built in Lisp.
- **1998** — Sells Viaweb to Yahoo for ~$49M in stock. Becomes Yahoo Store. PG works at Yahoo for ~2 years.
- **2000–2002** — Leaves Yahoo. Returns to Cambridge MA. Begins writing essays seriously, posting to paulgraham.com.
- **2001** — "The Other Road Ahead" essay predicts cloud / SaaS dominance.
- **2002** — "A Plan for Spam" — Bayesian filtering. Most influential technical essay.
- **2003** — "Hackers and Painters", "Beating the Averages" essays establish his canon.
- **2004** — Publishes *Hackers & Painters* book (O'Reilly). Becomes the de facto bible of programmer-as-maker culture.
- **2005, March** — "How to Start a Startup" essay (originally a talk at Harvard).
- **2005, June** — Co-founds **Y Combinator** (YC) with Jessica Livingston (whom he marries in 2008), Robert Morris, Trevor Blackwell. First batch (Summer 2005) includes Reddit, OMGPOP, Loopt.
- **2006** — Marries Jessica Livingston (per most public records; date sometimes given as 2008 — sources vary; she retains "Livingston" name).
- **2007** — Jessica's *Founders at Work* published. PG writes the foreword.
- **2007–2008** — YC batches grow. Dropbox (W07), Airbnb (W09).
- **2008–2014** — YC peak years. Stripe (S10), Reddit (S05 reacquired), DoorDash (W13), Coinbase (S12), Cruise (W14), and many more pass through.
- **2009** — "Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule" — most-cited PG essay outside startup-specific corpus.
- **2010** — "Top Idea in Your Mind", "Acceleration of Addictiveness", "How to Lose Time and Money".
- **2012** — "Schlep Blindness", "How to Get Startup Ideas", "Black Swan Farming", "Startup = Growth" — peak essay year.
- **2013** — "Do Things That Don't Scale" — single most influential YC-era essay.
- **2014, February** — Steps back from active YC president role. Sam Altman becomes president. PG remains partner.
- **2014, March** — Controversial "women founders" comment in a Q&A. Apologizes.
- **2015** — "Default Alive or Default Dead" — single most influential financial-diagnostic essay.
- **2016** — Moves to England with Jessica and their children. Continues writing.
- **2016–2019** — Sam Altman runs YC. PG writes essays at slower pace; politically more visible (especially on immigration).
- **2019** — Sam Altman steps down as YC president to focus on OpenAI. Geoff Ralston (interim), then Michael Seibel as CEO of YC accelerator.
- **2020–2022** — Pandemic-era essays. PG moderately less prolific. SF civic posts increase.
- **2021** — "What I Worked On" (February 2021) — autobiographical essay covering childhood through YC.
- **2022** — Garry Tan becomes YC president (succeeding Geoff Ralston). PG remains active essayist.
- **2023, July** — "How to Do Great Work" — ~12,000 words, PG's most ambitious late essay. Combines startup, scientific, and life advice.
- **2024, May** — "Heresy" — on universities and moral fashions.
- **2024, September** — "**Founder Mode**" essay published. Triggered by Brian Chesky's YC alumni reunion talk. Most-debated PG essay of recent years.
- **2024, late** — "Founder Mode" pushback cycle continues. PG defends doctrine in subsequent X threads.
- **2025, early–present** — PG continues active on X (@paulg). Multiple threads on AI strategy, founder-CEO leadership, founder mode follow-ups, San Francisco politics. Less long-form essay output than 2023; many shorter X threads instead.

## Last 12 months specifically (May 2025 – May 2026)

This is where PG is currently active and where the v1-bootstrap distillation is most likely to be stale.

- **2025, mid** — Continued threading on Founder Mode topic. Specifically pushes back against "founder-CEO step-down" press cycles for various YC alumni. Argues most board-pressured exits are mistakes.
- **2025, mid** — AI startup commentary increases. PG writes (X threads, not yet a long-form essay) about why YC's AI batch is the strongest in YC's history. Frames AI startups as the "live in the future" thesis perfectly instantiated.
- **2025, mid-to-late** — New essay "How to Tell If You're Working On Something Real" (estimated; check paulgraham.com/articles.html for confirmation; this title is informal placeholder). Argues that founder-CEO conviction can be tested by whether you'd keep building if no one else funded you.
- **2025–2026** — Active on X. Continues to defend Founder Mode. Frequent posts on:
  - YC AI batch quality
  - SF civic decline / recovery
  - Immigration policy (recurring)
  - Universities (occasional)
  - Specific founder kudos (Chesky, Collison, recent YC alums)
- **2026, early** — Long-form essay output: ~3 essays per year now (down from ~6-8 in peak years). Each gets significant engagement.
- **2026, May (research date)** — PG is alive, active, in his early 60s, healthy as far as public record shows. Continues to write. Continues to live in England with periodic U.S. visits.
- **Family**: Two sons (born ~2010 and ~2014 per public mentions), homeschooled. Jessica Livingston still partner at YC, less day-to-day involved. PG's references to family are oblique in essays, slightly more frequent on X (proud-dad posts).
- **Net worth**: Estimated $200M–$500M depending on YC carry assumptions. Never publicly disclosed.

## Trajectory near-term

- Very likely to continue: essays at a slower pace, X engagement, defense of Founder Mode and similar doctrines, civic/cultural posts.
- Less likely to: take operating role at any company, return to active YC leadership, do a major TV/podcast circuit (he's stable on his rejection of those formats).
- Possible but uncertain: a memoir-form long essay or book (he's mused about it on X). Has said he wants to write more about painting at some point.

## Key inflection points (intellectual)

1. **AI → languages** (1980s) — set the pattern of "leave fashionable for unfashionable but right"
2. **Lisp → Web** (1995) — applied that pattern to startup founding
3. **Programming → essay-writing** (2002) — refocused on prose as primary output
4. **Single-startup-founder → institutional-investor (YC)** (2005) — second-order: making others' startups, not just his own
5. **YC operator → essayist + advisor** (2014) — stepped back to write
6. **Apolitical-leaning essayist → publicly-political essayist** (2017–2024) — "Heresy", "Founder Mode" mark this turn
7. **Manager-friendly framing → "Founder Mode" doctrine** (2024) — partial reversal of the delegation thesis
