# Agent 1 — Paul Graham: Writings

## Books published

- **Hackers & Painters: Big Ideas from the Computer Age** (O'Reilly, 2004) — collected essays. Central thesis: programmers are makers in the lineage of artists; the Lisp tradition + good taste produce both. Repurposes "hack" from pejorative to honorific. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **On Lisp** (Prentice Hall, 1993) — macros and bottom-up design. Out of print; PG hosts free PDF at paulgraham.com/onlisp.html. Central technical thesis: the right way to write Lisp programs is to grow the language toward your problem. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **ANSI Common Lisp** (Prentice Hall, 1995) — textbook with rigorous reference appendix. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=2 total=11]

## Long-form essay corpus (paulgraham.com/articles.html)

PG's essays are the densest single-author body of startup writing. Count: ~220 essays, 1996–2025. URL pattern paulgraham.com/[slug].html. Sample of canonical ≥10-score primary sources:

### Founder / startup canon (2005–2014, peak YC era)

- **"How to Start a Startup"** (March 2005) — paulgraham.com/start.html. Three things: good people, make something customers want, spend as little money as possible. The ur-essay; YC's founding doctrine. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"How to Make Wealth"** (April 2004) — paulgraham.com/wealth.html. Wealth ≠ money; small group + measurable output + leverage = startup. Recurring framework for "why startups exist." [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Do Things That Don't Scale"** (July 2013) — paulgraham.com/ds.html. Counter to MBA "scalable from day one." Manually onboard, hand-write emails, deliver mattresses (Airbnb). Cited verbatim in every YC batch since. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Default Alive or Default Dead?"** (October 2015) — paulgraham.com/aord.html. Chart current burn against current growth — does runway end before profitability? Diagnostic question for any startup taking growth funding. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"How to Get Startup Ideas"** (November 2012) — paulgraham.com/startupideas.html. Don't think of startup ideas; notice them. Live in the future and build what's missing. Founders should be users of their own future. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Schlep Blindness"** (January 2012) — paulgraham.com/schlep.html. The most valuable startup ideas are hidden behind unpleasant work that founders unconsciously route around. Stripe is the canonical example. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Startup = Growth"** (September 2012) — paulgraham.com/growth.html. A startup is a company designed to grow fast. Not all small businesses are startups. The 5%-7%-10% weekly growth taxonomy. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Black Swan Farming"** (September 2012) — paulgraham.com/swan.html. Returns are concentrated in 1–2 outliers per batch. Therefore avoid eliminating any startup that has tail-end potential. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule"** (July 2009) — paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html. Maker hours come in 4-hour blocks; meetings cost half a day. PG's most cited essay outside YC. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Cities and Ambition"** (May 2008) — paulgraham.com/cities.html. Each city sends a different message ("be more ambitious", "be more creative"). NYC = money, SF = startups, Cambridge = ideas. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Why Smart People Have Bad Ideas"** (April 2005) — paulgraham.com/bronze.html. Smart ≠ correct about markets. Domain knowledge beats raw IQ in startup land. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"How to Convince Investors"** (August 2013) — paulgraham.com/convince.html. The way to convince is to have a startup that's actually working — not to be a better salesperson. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"What Startups Are Really Like"** (October 2009) — surveys ~100 YC alumni, distills ~17 surprises. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

### Hacker / craft canon (1993–2004, pre-YC)

- **"Beating the Averages"** (April 2003) — paulgraham.com/avg.html. Lisp is a secret weapon; Viaweb won because competitors couldn't match its speed of iteration. Origin of "Blub paradox": a programmer in language X can't see why X is missing. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Hackers and Painters"** (May 2003) — paulgraham.com/hp.html. Programming is making, not science. The CS curriculum mistake is teaching it as math. Defends hacker culture against the engineer-bureaucrat drift. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"What You Can't Say"** (January 2004) — paulgraham.com/say.html. Identify the moral fashions of your time before you defend them. Test: would you be persecuted for saying X in a different era? [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"The Other Road Ahead"** (September 2001) — paulgraham.com/road.html. Web apps will eat desktop apps. Written from Viaweb experience, before SaaS was a category. Predicts the cloud, calls it "server-based software." [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"A Plan for Spam"** (August 2002) — paulgraham.com/spam.html. Bayesian filters against spam. PG's most cited *technical* essay; spawned a generation of ML-spam-filter startups. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

### Late-period (2014–2025)

- **"Founder Mode"** (September 2024) — paulgraham.com/foundermode.html. The most controversial recent essay. Founders should NOT delegate the way "manager mode" textbooks say; instead they should engage at every level, "skip-level meetings," "hire only people you trust deeply." Triggered by Brian Chesky's lecture at YC alum reunion. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"How to Do Great Work"** (July 2023) — paulgraham.com/greatwork.html. ~12,000 words. PG's most ambitious late essay. Combines founder advice, scientific advice, and life advice. Choose work you have natural aptitude for, deep interest in, and can do great work in. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"How to Lose Time and Money"** (July 2010) — paulgraham.com/selfindulgence.html. Small drips of self-indulgence beat dramatic disasters. Most failures come from prolonged comfort, not single bad decisions. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"The Acceleration of Addictiveness"** (July 2010) — paulgraham.com/addiction.html. Tech makes things more addictive. Personal rules are the only defense. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"The Top of My Todo List"** (April 2012) — paulgraham.com/top.html. Five things people regret on their deathbed. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Heresy"** (May 2024) — paulgraham.com/heresy.html. On the ideological climate of universities, woke as a moral fashion. [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]
- **"Crazy New Ideas"** (April 2021) — paulgraham.com/newideas.html. Genuine breakthroughs sound crazy at first; this is downstream of "live in the future." [P=3 V=3 A=3 C=3 total=12]

## Books / thinkers PG cites approvingly (intellectual lineage)

- **John McCarthy** (Lisp; PG's intellectual father)
- **Richard Stallman, Eric Raymond** ("hacker" tradition; Cathedral and the Bazaar referenced obliquely)
- **Robert Pirsig** (*Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance* — quality framing)
- **Edwin Land** (Polaroid; also a Steve Jobs touchstone — convergent intellectual taste)
- **Galileo, Newton, Darwin** — recurring as exemplars of "great work"
- **Ramanujan, Erdős, Feynman** — the lone-genius / collaborator templates in "How to Do Great Work"
- **Montaigne** — essay tradition; PG explicitly aspires to this lineage in *Hackers & Painters* preface
- **Bertrand Russell** (precision of prose, refusal of pomposity)
- **George Orwell** ("Politics and the English Language" — explicitly PG's prose ideal; cited in "Writing, Briefly" 2005)
- **William Strunk, E. B. White** (*Elements of Style*)
- **Jane Jacobs** (cities; cited in "Cities and Ambition")

PG explicitly does NOT cite: management literature (Drucker, Christensen except in passing), pop-business (Gladwell, Brooks), modern academic philosophy, postmodern critics. He writes as if the management-canon doesn't exist.

## Notes on tone evolution

- **Early-PG (1996–2004)**: strident, contrarian on Lisp/CS curriculum, anti-Java, anti-corporate. Reads as a programmer who's been frustrated.
- **Middle-PG (2005–2014)**: YC operating manual mode — patient, instructive, anecdote-driven, careful with claims. The peak teaching voice.
- **Late-PG (2015–2025)**: more confident on culture/politics, less confident on tech specifics. "Founder Mode" feels like a return to the early stridency. The tone of someone who has made his money and is tired of being polite.
