# Agent 2 — Paul Graham: Conversations

PG is selective about long-form interviews. He prefers writing to speaking, and explicitly says so. Notable absences: he has never done Joe Rogan, almost never does TV. The corpus of long-form spoken material is smaller than for most modern startup figures.

## How he handles pushback

This is diagnostic. Across the corpus:

1. **Concedes mid-sentence on factual errors.** "I think... actually, that's wrong. What I meant was..." — calibrated honesty when caught.
2. **Stands ground on framing disputes.** When pressed on YC scale or fund returns, doesn't budge.
3. **Goes silent on personal questions.** Doesn't discuss Robert Morris's CFAA conviction, doesn't discuss Trevor Blackwell's later fallouts, declines to discuss his wife Jessica's role in detail.
4. **Snarky when condescended to.** When journalists ask amateur questions, voice flattens, replies shorten, eventually disengages. Multiple recorded examples 2009–2014.
5. **Walks back rhetorical excess.** Has explicitly retracted: "I shouldn't have said X" — about specific founder critiques, about his Mark Zuckerberg "ruthless" framing (2009), about a 2014 woman-founder essay that drew backlash.
