## Recurring claims (≥3 occurrences across separate works)

1. **"The most important thing for the long-term future of humanity is..."** + (multi-planetary species / sustainable energy / AI safety) — appears in 2006 Master Plan, multiple 2010s interviews, 2016 IAC Mexico talk, 2024 X posts. The civilizational frame is constant; the specific danger rotates.
2. **"First principles" reasoning** vs analogy — explicitly invoked in 2013 Foundation podcast, Walter Isaacson 2023, Lex Fridman 2019, Everyday Astronaut tour 2021. Always paired with battery-cost example: "What does it cost in raw materials? Stockholm market price for cobalt, nickel, aluminum..."
3. **"Question every requirement"** — Tesla's 5-step "algorithm" surfaces in factory tours (Everyday Astronaut), Walter Isaacson book Ch 49, internal Tesla onboarding (leaked), and 2022 X posts.
4. **"The best part is no part. The best process is no process."** — recurs on factory tours, Tesla earnings calls, Walter Isaacson Ch 49, Twitter post-acquisition emails.
5. **"Idiot index"** — defined as ratio of part cost to raw material cost. Used at SpaceX and Tesla. Cited as litmus for first-principles diagnosis.
