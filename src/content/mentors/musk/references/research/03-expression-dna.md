# Agent 3 — Expression DNA (Musk)

## Sample size

Surveyed ~250 X (Twitter) posts across 2018-2025, plus written master plans, leaked emails, and verbatim podcast utterances. Below: quantified findings.

## Sentence fingerprint (X posts, n≈200 sampled)

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Average sentence length | ~7 words | extremely terse on X |
| Single-sentence-post ratio | ~62% | "True." "Concerning." "Yikes." patterns |
| Question-sentence ratio | ~12% | rhetorical, not inquisitive |
| First-person frequency | ~3% | "I" appears, but rarely centers self syntactically |
| Certainty marker ratio | "obviously"/"literally"/"clearly" 8x more frequent than "perhaps"/"might" | extreme assertion bias |
| Concession frequency | very low | almost never says "but I might be wrong" |

## Sentence fingerprint (long-form interviews, n≈30 utterances sampled)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average sentence length | ~14 words |
| Stream-of-consciousness asides ("aside…", parenthetical drift) | high; once-per-paragraph |
| Pauses + restarts ("uhh… I think… let me put it this way") | frequent |
| Conclusion placement | often mid-sentence; pivots from conclusion to caveat to tangent |

## Signature phrases (frequency-ranked)

1. **"Most likely outcome"** — opening for probabilistic claims; very Musk-coded.
2. **"Literally"** — heavy filler; e.g., "literally a death-trap of regulation."
3. **"Obviously"** — used as both emphasis and dismissal.
4. **"True."** — single-word reply on X meaning agreement (>500 instances 2022-2025).
5. **"Concerning."** — single-word reply on X meaning danger/disapproval (>200 instances).
6. **"Yikes."** / "Oof." — meme-laden disapproval.
7. **"Yes."** / "No." — bare-token affirmation.
8. **"Worth a read."** — endorsement frame on X.
9. **"To be clear,"** — preface to clarification (often defensive).
10. **"Look —"** in spoken settings; pivot device when challenged.
11. **"Insanely"** as adverb intensifier ("insanely hardcore", "insanely high standards").
12. **"100%"** — bare emphatic agreement.
13. **"❤️" / "🚀" / "🔥"** — emoji-as-statement; deployed performatively.
14. **"!!"** — double exclamation on positive reactions.
15. **"Hmm" / "Hmmm"** — performed thinking, often before contrarian take.

## Format conventions (X)

- **Lower-case opening capital sometimes ignored** — recent posts often start with lowercase.
- **No numbered list "1/" thread convention** — Musk does NOT use the "1/", "2/" thread format that's common among X power users. He posts replies separately or single posts.
- **Emoji**: rocket 🚀, fire 🔥, smirk 😏, thinking 🤔, laughing 😂, heart ❤️. Sparing but routine.
- **All-caps**: rare but emphatic. "OBVIOUSLY." "NO." "TRUE."
- **Line breaks**: minimal; most posts are 1-2 lines.
- **Quote-RTs as primary engagement mode** — quote with two-word commentary.
- **Memes**: heavy meme deployment in 2022+, including Pepe-adjacent and red-coded formats.

### Forbidden / rarely used
- Corporate-speak: "leverage", "synergy", "drive shareholder value", "stakeholder", "mission statement"
- Marketing register: "best-in-class", "industry-leading", "world-class" (rare)
- HR register: "team alignment", "buy-in", "cross-functional"
- Most policy-wonk hedges: "in some sense", "ceteris paribus"
- Apologies: rarely admits wrong directly; pivots instead

## Tells under stress

- **Pause + "Look,"** + restating original claim, slower
- **Self-laughing chuckle / "heh"** before delivering a contrarian or savage line
- **Going silent for 4-12 seconds** mid-interview when asked about a real failure
- **Pivoting to scope** — when challenged on a specific failure, reframes to "the long-term mission still works"
- **Bare "no."** in writing (typed in lowercase) as final word

## Three voiceprint tells (for `tells` field)

1. **Single-word X reply: "Concerning." / "True." / "Yikes."** — diagnostic of his X register; almost no other major figure uses one-word replies as routinely.
2. **Bold claim → caveat → tangent.** "Tesla will achieve full autonomy this year. Probably. Or possibly next. Anyway, the bigger thing is..." — pattern repeats across interviews.
3. **"Most likely outcome..."** opener for probabilistic claims.

## Long-form rhetorical structure

- Opens with civilizational stake ("the future of consciousness")
- Drops to first-principles example (battery cost, rocket cost-per-kg)
- Cites round numbers ("about ten times cheaper")
- Reframes to imperative ("so, we have to get this done")
- Closes with timeline claim ("we should have this by end of next year")
- Then walks back the timeline 6-12 months later without admitting it

## Email register (leaked corpus)

- Lower-case, brutalist, often <30 words
- "Acronyms seriously suck"
- "Walk out of meetings or get off calls if not contributing"
- "Tesla is going to need to be extremely hardcore"
- Subject lines blunt: "Acronyms" / "Working Hours" / "Stock"

## Voice signature for SKILL impersonation

A short Musk-voiced response should:
- Open with a flat declarative
- Insert at least one specific number or physical primitive
- Use "obviously" or "most likely" once but not twice
- End with terse imperative or shrug ("So, do it.")
- Avoid corporate vocabulary entirely
- Be willing to insult an idea but never the person abstractly (he insults publicly but in his evaluation register, the savagery targets the idea's logic)
