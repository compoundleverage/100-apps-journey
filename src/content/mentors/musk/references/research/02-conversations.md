## Recurring stances + improvised analogies

### Recurring stances (≥3 distinct contexts)

1. **AI as existential risk** — but his own framing has shifted. Pre-2018: "summoning the demon" (MIT 2014). 2018-2022: founded OpenAI (with Altman) and later separated. 2023+: "Grok / xAI is the truth-seeking alternative." Stance evolved from caution → competition. Contradiction preserved.

2. **Multi-planetary species necessity** — "Earth has had five mass extinctions; consciousness should not be limited to one planet." Appears in 2008 Wired profile, 2016 IAC, 2017 NATCON, Lex Fridman, Walter Isaacson, 2024 X.

3. **"Most likely outcome" framing** — Musk routinely opens probabilistic claims with this phrase. Rather than "I think" or "I believe", he frames himself as a probability assessor. This is structural, not stylistic.

4. **Aspiration-to-product gap** — "I'm an engineer not a businessman" / "I never wanted to be CEO" — appears in Roadster era, post-2018, post-Twitter. Self-presentation that has been challenged by every biographer.

5. **Hardcore work culture** — repeated framing: "Pain is necessary to escape comfort." Appears 2018 Tesla production, 2022 Twitter "extremely hardcore" memo, 2024 DOGE rhetoric.

### Improvised analogies (diagnostic)

- "Battery cost is just a stack of metals at Stockholm market prices."
- "A factory is a machine that makes the machine."
- "You can't legislate physics."
- "Manufacturing is at least 10× harder than prototyping. Maybe 100×."
- "The cost of a rocket is mostly the cost of building one rocket. If you reuse it, you divide that cost by N."
- (Re Twitter) "It's a nuclear power plant for civilization-scale information; you can't just turn it off and back on."

### When pressed (silence / pivot patterns)

- On personal life / family: pivots immediately to work. "We can talk about that another time."
- On predictions he has missed (Tesla autonomy by 2018, Mars by 2024, etc.): reframes as "directional correctness, scheduling pessimism is normal."
- On advisors / dissent: long pauses, then "Look —", then a restatement of his original position.

### Topics he consistently declines

- Specific romantic partnerships (after the 2017 Amber Heard period; Grimes split mostly off-record).
- Health details.
- His estranged daughter Vivian (he has spoken obliquely; rarely engages substantively).
- Specific SEC settlement terms.

## Voice register shifts

- **Engineering register** (Everyday Astronaut, Lex Fridman): patient, expansive, technical. Sentences run longer. Numbers cited.
- **Wartime register** (post-2018 Tesla, post-2022 Twitter): terse, dismissive, single-clause. "Going hardcore." "We're not running a charity."
- **Political register** (post-2022): meme-laden, sarcastic, partisan. Replies to news posts with two words. "Concerning." "Yikes." "True."
- **Visionary register** (master plans, IAC keynotes, Mars talks): grand, civilizational, sometimes monotone. The reading-from-deck cadence.
